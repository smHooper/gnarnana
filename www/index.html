The Race

The Gnarnana 50 main event is a packraft race from Mile 117 of the Denali Highway to Kingfisher Creek near the Denali Park entrance on the Nenana River. This Class II-III stretch through the middle of the Alaska Range offers a mix of everything from challenging rapids to scenic flat water.

Racers must:

  • Travel in a team of two or more
  • Paddle single-person packraft
  • Carry at least one satellite communication device (e.g., sat phone, inReach), one first aid kit, and one repair kit per team
  • Possess the skill to safely paddle high-volume Class III whitewater

Want to help out? This community-building event will require the help of many. If you want to lend a hand, give us a shout at denalipackraft@gmail.com. We're looking for volunteers for:

  • Registration
  • Race start help and sweep boaters
  • Setting safety at challenging rapids
  • Staffing checkpoint/aid stations

Logistics

Camping

Camping at the Longhorn Saloon is $25 per person per night. If you wish to camp somewhere else, please still stop by one of the 2 locations for registration. Online registration will also be available, however, you will still need to come by one of the 2 locations to get race numbers, verify payment of $50 for the race, and get your team number.

Race Details

The Race will begin at 7am. Teams will leave every 5 minutes depending on how many teams are registered. Teams will be asked to gauge the speed of their team. Faster teams will start later, and teams that think they will need more time will start earlier.

Race participants are encouraged to carpool to Mile Marker 117 of the Denali Highway for the start of the race. Please be there at least 30 minutes prior to the start of your heat so that you can get your gear prepared for racing.

The Race will go from 7am-7pm. Any team that has not reached the Crabbie's Crossing bridge (second bridge you will cross during the race) by 5pm will have to exit the race.

Race Shuttle

New Wave Adventures will shuttle race participants from Kingfisher Creek, back to the Denali Highway/Longhorn Saloon once the race finishes around 7pm. Teams are welcome to set up their own shuttle for the race, but need to check in once they are off the water so everyone is accounted for.
